AI insights

How VEGA uses your pet's logged history to surface patterns worth discussing with your vet.

AI insights are not diagnoses. They are pattern suggestions based on logged data, intended to prompt a conversation with a qualified veterinarian. Never use them to self-diagnose or delay professional care.

What AI insights do

VEGA's AI service analyses your pet's health log history to detect statistical patterns associated with common conditions. For cats, this currently includes early indicators of Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) — such as sustained weight loss combined with reduced appetite over several weeks.

When a pattern is detected, VEGA shows a finding in the Insights tab with:

  • The condition flagged and a confidence level.
  • The specific data points that triggered the finding (e.g. "weight loss trend over 6 weeks", "appetite rated reduced on 12 of the last 20 logs").
  • A plain-language recommendation — typically to schedule a vet appointment.

What AI insights don't do

  • They do not diagnose your pet.
  • They are not a substitute for a physical examination or blood tests.
  • A finding does not mean your pet definitely has the flagged condition — it means the logged data resembles patterns associated with it.
  • A lack of findings does not mean your pet is healthy. VEGA only detects patterns it has been trained to recognise.

How to enable AI insights

AI insights require two things:

  1. Cloud sync enabled. Your logs need to reach our servers so the AI service can analyse them. Enable this in Settings → Data & Privacy → Cloud sync.
  2. AI training consent granted. Once sync is on, VEGA will ask separately whether you'd like to contribute anonymised data to improve the models. This is independent of sync — you can say no and still receive insights based on your own data once the feature supports this configuration.

Both can be revoked at any time from Settings → Data & Privacy.

Your data and the AI service

When your logs are sent for analysis, the AI service receives only:

  • Your pet's species.
  • The log entries (type, timestamp, values).
  • A random internal identifier — never your name, your pet's name, or your email address.

No personally identifiable information ever reaches the AI service. See our Privacy Policy for the full picture.

Why consistent logging matters

The AI service works better with more data points. A single weight entry tells it very little. Weekly weight entries over two months give it a trend to work with. The more consistently you log, the more meaningful any findings will be.
