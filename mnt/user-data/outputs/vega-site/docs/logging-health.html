Logging health data

What you can record, and why consistent logging makes the AI insights more useful.

Adding a log entry

Open a pet's profile and tap Add log. Choose the log type, fill in the relevant fields, add any free-text notes, and tap Save. The entry is stored instantly on your device.

Log types

TypeWhat to recordKey fields
WeightYour pet's body weightWeight in kg
AppetiteHow much and how eagerly they ateRating (good / reduced / refused), notes
MedicationA dose givenDrug name, dose in mg, route (oral / topical / injection)
MoodGeneral energy and demeanourRating (normal / low / lethargic / hyperactive), notes
SymptomsAny observed symptomDescription, severity, duration
Vet visitA consultation or procedureClinic name, vet name, reason, outcome, next appointment
VaccinationA vaccine administeredVaccine name, batch number, next due date
CustomAnything elseLabel + free text — useful for breed-specific observations

Editing and deleting logs

Tap any log entry in the Logs list to open it. Use the edit icon (✏️) to correct a mistake, or the delete icon (🗑) to remove it. Deleted entries cannot be recovered.

Viewing history

The Logs screen shows all entries in reverse chronological order. Use the filter button to narrow down by log type, or scroll the weight entries to see a trend over time.

Tips for useful logging

  • Weigh regularly. Even a weekly weight entry builds a trend the AI can work with. Weight loss is one of the earliest detectable signals for conditions like CKD.
  • Log appetite every day if you can. Appetite changes are often the first thing owners notice before a diagnosis.
  • Record medications as you give them. The Logs screen becomes a reliable medication history for your vet.
  • Use the notes field freely. Observations that don't fit a category still matter — write them in a Custom log or the notes field of any entry.
Logging health data is not a substitute for veterinary care. If you notice something concerning, contact your vet — don't wait to see what the app says.
